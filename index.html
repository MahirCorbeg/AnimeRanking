Top 10 Most Watched & Popular Anime Franchises of All Time

Explore the most iconic anime series that shaped generations, broke viewership records, and defined the genre globally.

#1: Dragon Ball

Years: 1986 – Present

Total Episodes: 800+ (combined across DB, DBZ, Super, GT)

About: One of the most influential anime franchises in history. Dragon Ball follows Goku’s journey from childhood to universal battles. Its cultural impact, unforgettable characters, and long-lasting popularity make it the most watched anime globally.

#2: One Piece

Years: 1999 – Present

Total Episodes: 1,100+ (still ongoing)

About: One Piece tells the epic tale of Monkey D. Luffy and his pirate crew in search of the ultimate treasure. Known for its emotional depth, world-building, and unmatched longevity, it holds a record for the most manga volumes sold worldwide.

#3: Naruto

Years: 2002 – 2017

Total Episodes: 720 (220 in Naruto, 500 in Naruto: Shippuden)

About: Naruto follows the journey of a young ninja, Naruto Uzumaki, as he seeks recognition and dreams of becoming the Hokage. With deep themes of friendship, struggle, and redemption, it grew into one of the most beloved anime series worldwide.

#4: Attack on Titan

Years: 2013 – 2023

Total Episodes: 87

About: In a world ravaged by giant man-eating Titans, humanity fights for survival behind massive walls. Eren Yeager and his friends join the military to uncover the truth and reclaim freedom. Known for its intense story, plot twists, and philosophical depth, AoT became a global sensation.

#5: Pokémon

Years: 1997 – Present

Total Episodes: 1200+

About: Pokémon follows Ash Ketchum on his journey to become a Pokémon Master. With global influence across games, merchandise, and media, it’s one of the most recognizable anime franchises in the world — capturing generations with its charm and sense of adventure.

#6: Death Note

Years: 2006 – 2007

Total Episodes: 37

About: When genius student Light Yagami discovers a notebook that can kill anyone whose name is written in it, he sets out to create a new world free of crime — but is met with the mysterious detective L. A gripping psychological thriller that blurs the line between justice and madness.

#7: Demon Slayer

Years: 2019 – Present

Total Episodes: 55+ (ongoing)

About: Tanjiro Kamado becomes a demon slayer after his family is slaughtered by demons and his sister Nezuko is turned into one. Known for its breathtaking animation and emotional storytelling, Demon Slayer became a cultural phenomenon in Japan and worldwide.

#8: Sword Art Online

Years: 2012 – Present

Total Episodes: 96+

About: Set in a virtual MMORPG where players are trapped and death in-game means death in real life, SAO follows Kirito’s journey through several game worlds. A blend of romance, action, and futuristic storytelling made this series extremely popular among younger audiences.

#9: My Hero Academia

Years: 2016 – Present

Total Episodes: 140+

About: In a world where nearly everyone has a superpower known as a “quirk,” Izuku Midoriya is born without one. His journey to become the greatest hero is both emotional and action-packed. A massive hit globally, especially among younger anime fans and comic book lovers.

#10: Bleach

Years: 2004 – 2012 / 2022 – Present

Total Episodes: 390+ (including Thousand-Year Blood War arc)

About: Ichigo Kurosaki accidentally obtains the powers of a Soul Reaper — a celestial being who protects the living world from evil spirits. With epic sword fights, soul society lore, and comeback arcs, Bleach holds a legendary status in anime history.
